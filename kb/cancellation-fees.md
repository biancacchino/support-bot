# Cancellation fees

Most cancellations cost nothing.
A fee only applies when we have already done work that we cannot undo.

## When there is no fee

You are not charged anything if:

- You cancel while the order is still **Confirmed**, which means picking has not started.
- You refuse the delivery at the door.
- We cancel the order, for example because a line is out of stock and you did not want a substitute.
- The order is cancelled because we missed our delivery window.

In practice this covers the large majority of cancellations.
If you cancel the same day you ordered, and you ordered for a delivery more than 48 hours out, there is effectively never a fee.

## When a fee applies

A fee applies once the order has moved to **Being picked**, because at that point stock has been pulled, packed, and in some cases put into temperature-controlled packaging that cannot be reused.

| Goods | Fee once picking has started |
|---|---|
| Ambient (dry goods, tinned, confectionery) | 10% of the cancelled line value, capped at £25 |
| Chilled and dairy | 25% of the cancelled line value |
| Frozen and seafood | 25% of the cancelled line value |
| Special order and imported seasonal | Up to 100%, see below |

The fee is charged on the value of the lines you cancelled, not on the whole order.

## Special orders

Lines we buy in specifically for you, including imported seasonal produce and anything marked **Special order** at checkout, are the exception.

Once we have committed the order to the producer, we cannot return it, and the fee can be the full line value.
Whether it is depends on whether the producer will take it back, which we will find out and tell you before charging anything.

We never charge a special-order fee without telling you the amount first and giving you the chance to keep the goods instead.

## How the fee is charged

The fee is deducted from your refund rather than billed separately.
If you paid £400 for chilled goods and cancel after picking, you are refunded £300 and the £100 fee is retained.

Your refund confirmation email itemises the fee, so you can always see what was deducted and why.

## Disputing a fee

If you think a fee was applied wrongly, for example because you cancelled before picking started but were charged anyway, contact us with your order number.

Timestamps are logged on every status change, so this is usually quick to resolve.
If the log shows picking had not started when you cancelled, the fee is refunded in full.
