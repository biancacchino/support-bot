# Closing your account

## Before you can close

An account cannot be closed while it has anything outstanding.
Specifically:

- No orders in progress. Wait for them to be delivered, or cancel them.
- No open refunds. These have to conclude first, since we cannot refund into a closed account.
- No unpaid balance if you are on credit terms. The statement must be settled.
- No account credit remaining. Spend it or ask us to pay it out, because it is forfeited on closure.

That last one catches people.
If you have credit on the account, closing it loses the money.
Check before you close.

## Closing it

Sign in, go to **Account settings**, and choose **Close account**.

You will be asked to confirm, and then the account closes at the end of that day.
You can cancel the closure at any point before it completes by signing in again.

If you are on credit terms, closure requires a human to confirm the final statement is settled, so it goes into a review queue rather than closing the same day.

## What happens to your data

**Deleted within 30 days:** your sign-in credentials, contact details, notification preferences, saved baskets, and delivery addresses.

**Retained for 7 years:** invoices, credit notes, and the order records behind them.

We keep the second set because we are legally required to.
UK tax law requires us to retain business records for six years after the end of the accounting period they relate to, and we cannot delete them on request, even yours.

This means closing your account does not erase your order history from our records.
It removes your ability to sign in and see it, and it removes the personal data that is not part of a financial record.

## Reopening

For 30 days after closure, contact us and we can reopen the account exactly as it was.

After 30 days the sign-in data is genuinely gone and you would need to register again from scratch, including going through verification again.
Your old invoices would not reappear on the new account.

## If you just want to stop being emailed

You do not need to close the account to stop hearing from us.

Turn off notifications in **Account settings**.
A dormant account costs you nothing, has no fees, and does not expire, and it means your order history and any credit stay available if you come back.

Closing an account you might use again is almost always the wrong move.
Leaving it dormant is free.
