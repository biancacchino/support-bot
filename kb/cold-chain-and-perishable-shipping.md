# Cold chain and perishable shipping

Much of the Northwind range is chilled, frozen, or fresh.
These lines behave differently from dry goods at almost every step, and most perishable complaints come from not knowing that in advance.

## What counts as perishable

- **Chilled:** dairy, cheese, charcuterie, fresh pasta, prepared sauces.
- **Frozen:** seafood, frozen fruit, ice cream.
- **Fresh:** produce, herbs, bakery.

Anything temperature-controlled is flagged as **Chilled**, **Frozen**, or **Fresh** on the product page and in the basket.

## How they ship

Perishables ship in insulated packaging with coolant, sized to hold temperature for **up to 24 hours** from packing.

That 24-hour figure is the whole reason the rules below exist.
Everything about how these consignments are handled is designed to get them to you inside that window.

Consequently:

- They cannot go by standard delivery. Named day or faster only.
- They are picked and packed last, and dispatched early.
- They usually arrive before 10am.
- They carry a £6.00 cold-chain surcharge per consignment.
- They cannot be delivered to postcodes without a next-day service, and no surcharge changes that.

## Receiving a perishable delivery

**Somebody has to be there.**
A perishable consignment cannot be left with a neighbour, left in a safe place, or left in a porch.
If nobody is there to receive it, it comes back on the vehicle and is destroyed.

That is charged to you, and it is not refundable, because the failure was at your end.
This is the single most expensive mistake customers make with these lines.

**Check it before you sign.**
Every consignment has a temperature indicator inside the box. If it has been triggered, the cold chain has failed.

If the box is not cold to the touch, or the indicator has tripped, refuse the consignment or sign as damaged.
Do not sign clean for a warm box.

**Get it into storage immediately.**
Once you have accepted it, the cold chain is your responsibility.
A consignment that arrived cold and then sat on a loading bay for three hours is not something we can refund.

## If it arrives warm

Photograph the indicator, the packaging, and the goods, then raise a claim the **same day**.

The claim window for perishables is **24 hours** from delivery, much shorter than the 14 days for ambient goods, and it is short for a reason: after that we cannot tell a transit failure from a storage failure at your end.

Do not eat it, do not sell it, and do not throw it away until we authorise disposal.

We refund failed cold-chain consignments in full and do not ask for the goods back.

## Mixed orders

An order with both ambient and perishable lines ships together where possible.

Where it cannot, it part-dispatches: the perishables come on their own vehicle, often on a different day.
You are only charged one delivery fee, plus the single cold-chain surcharge.

Both halves show separately under **Orders**, each with their own status and tracking, which is why one order can show two tracking links.

## No cancellation once picked

Perishables cannot be cancelled once picking has started, and cannot be returned because you changed your mind.
Once they have left the cold store we cannot return them to saleable stock.
See the articles on cancelling an order and on the refund policy.
