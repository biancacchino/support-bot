# Creating an account

Northwind Traders is a wholesale supplier, so accounts are for businesses rather than individuals.

## What you need

- A registered business name and, if you have one, a company number.
- A VAT number if you are VAT registered.
- A business email address. Free email domains are accepted but will slow verification.
- A delivery address that someone can receive goods at during working hours.

You do not need a trade reference to open a standard account.
You do need one to apply for credit terms, which is a separate application.

## Signing up

Go to **Create account** and complete the form.
You will be asked to confirm your email address before you can sign in, and the confirmation link is valid for 24 hours.

Most accounts are approved automatically and are usable within a few minutes.

## Verification

Some accounts are held for manual verification, typically when:

- The business name does not match Companies House records.
- The VAT number does not validate.
- The delivery address is residential.

We are not being obstructive. Wholesale pricing is only available to trade, so we have to check that you are trade.

Manual verification takes up to 2 working days.
You can browse and build a basket while it is pending, but you cannot check out.

## Account types

**Standard.** Pay at checkout by card or PayPal. Available immediately on approval. This is what most new accounts start as.

**Credit terms.** Pay on 30-day terms against a statement. Requires a separate application, two trade references, and a credit check. Takes around 5 working days to assess.

You can start on a standard account and apply for terms later.
There is no advantage to waiting, so most customers open a standard account and trade on it while the credit application is processed.

## Multiple sites

If you operate more than one site, you have a choice.

Add multiple delivery addresses to a single account if the sites share purchasing and you want one statement.
Open separate accounts if the sites buy independently and need their own invoices and credit limits.

Most multi-site customers want the second option.
Moving from one to the other later is possible but tedious, so it is worth deciding up front.

## If it goes wrong
