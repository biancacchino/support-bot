# Delivery times and cut-offs

## Cut-off times

To get the delivery speed you paid for, the order has to be placed before the cut-off.

| Option | Order by | Arrives |
|---|---|---|
| Next day | 4pm | Next working day |
| Pre-9am | 3pm | Next working day, before 9am |
| Named day (chilled or frozen) | 2pm, day before | Your chosen day |
| Named day (ambient) | 6pm, day before | Your chosen day |
| Standard | Any time | 2 to 3 working days |

An order placed at 4:01pm for next-day delivery is treated as having been placed the following day.
The website shows a live countdown to the next cut-off, and it is the authority.

## Working days

Working days are **Monday to Friday, excluding public holidays**.

Weekends do not count.
An order placed on Friday afternoon for next-day delivery arrives on Monday, not Saturday.
A standard order placed on Friday arrives Tuesday or Wednesday.

We do not deliver on Saturdays or Sundays.

## Public holidays and Christmas

Bank holidays do not count as working days, and the effect compounds.
An order placed the Friday before a bank holiday weekend on next-day delivery arrives on the Tuesday.

Christmas is the one to plan around.
We publish a Christmas cut-off schedule each November, and the last chilled and frozen delivery is typically around 22 December, with nothing moving again until the first working day of January.

Chilled and frozen goods are not held over a shutdown.
If you order perishables for delivery into a closed period, they will not be stored and delivered later. The order will be moved to the next available date, which may be well into January.

## When the day starts

Deliveries run from **7am to 6pm**.

We cannot give you a time within that window on standard, named day, or next day.
Only pre-9am guarantees a time.

Chilled and frozen consignments are delivered **early**, typically before 10am, because the packaging holds temperature for a limited number of hours.
If your site cannot receive goods that early, set your delivery window on the address, because we plan routes around it.

## The courier's estimate

Once dispatched, the courier's tracking gives its own estimated window, usually on the morning of delivery.
That estimate is more accurate than anything we can tell you, since it is based on the actual route the driver is running.

## When it is late

A delivery is late once the working day has ended without it arriving.

Contact us if:

- A next-day or named-day order has not arrived by 6pm on the day it was due.
- A pre-9am order has not arrived by 9am.
- A standard order is more than one working day past its window.

For a missed guaranteed delivery, pre-9am or named day, the delivery charge is refunded.
For chilled and frozen goods that arrive late and warm, the goods are refunded too, and you should not accept them.
