# Downloading or requesting an invoice

## Downloading

Sign in, go to **Invoices**, and choose **Download PDF** against the invoice you want.

To download several at once, filter to the date range you want and choose **Download all**.
That gives you a zip of PDFs, plus a CSV summary, which is what most bookkeepers actually want at the end of a quarter.

Invoices are also available as CSV individually, for importing into accounting software.

## Getting invoices emailed automatically

You do not have to come and fetch them.

In **Account settings**, then **Notifications**, set an invoice recipient.
This can be a different address from the one that gets order confirmations, so invoices can go straight to your bookkeeper or to an accounts inbox while dispatch notifications go to whoever is receiving the goods.

You can set several recipients.
For credit-terms accounts you can choose to receive individual invoices, the monthly statement only, or both.
Most finance teams want the statement only, since the individual invoices just clutter the inbox.

## Requesting a copy

Every invoice is already available to download, so a request is only needed when you cannot sign in.

If the account is closed, or you no longer have access, contact us with the business name, the account number, and the invoice or order number.
We can send copies of anything within the 7-year retention window.

We send invoice copies only to an address already on the account, or to a verifiable company domain.
Invoices contain your trading and pricing information, so we are careful about where they go.

## Corrections and reissues

**We cannot edit an issued invoice.**
Once issued it is a financial record, and changing it after the fact is not something we are permitted to do.

If an invoice is wrong we issue a **credit note** cancelling it, then issue a corrected invoice.
You end up with three documents rather than one, and the credit note is what reconciles the difference.
This is normal accounting practice, not a sign anything has gone wrong.

Things we can correct this way: wrong goods invoiced, wrong price, wrong VAT treatment, wrong quantity.

## What we cannot change

**The billing entity on an issued invoice.**
If the invoice was issued to the wrong company, we credit it in full and reissue to the correct one, but only where the goods genuinely went to that entity.

We cannot reissue an invoice to a different company simply because it suits you for it to be billed elsewhere.
That would be a false document, and we will decline it however the request is phrased.

**The invoice date.**
It is the date of dispatch. We cannot backdate or forward-date it into a different VAT period.

## VAT numbers added late

If you add and verify a VAT number after an invoice was issued, we cannot retrospectively re-rate old invoices.
The VAT treatment is fixed at the point of issue, and reclaiming is a matter between you and HMRC.
