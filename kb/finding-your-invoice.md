# Finding your invoice

## Where invoices live

Sign in and go to **Invoices**.
Every invoice we have ever issued to the account is there, searchable by invoice number, order number, date range, or amount.

Invoices are retained for **7 years**, so historic ones remain available long after the order itself has scrolled out of view.
This is true even for a closed account, though you would need to ask us for them rather than sign in.

## When the invoice is issued

**The invoice is created on dispatch, not when you order.**

This is the single most common invoice question.
If you placed an order this morning and there is no invoice, that is expected: the order has not shipped yet.

- Standard accounts: invoice issued when the order is dispatched.
- Credit-terms accounts: individual invoices issued on dispatch, then consolidated into a monthly statement.

For part-dispatched orders you get one invoice per dispatch, so a single order number can have two or three invoices against it.

## Reading the invoice

Each invoice shows the invoice number (`INV-` followed by six digits), the order number it relates to, the goods, VAT, and delivery.

Zero-rated and standard-rated goods are shown separately, because most food is zero-rated for VAT but confectionery, alcoholic drinks, crisps, and hot food are not.
A mixed order will show both, and the split is not an error.

## The invoice does not match what I paid

Usually one of these:

**Short-shipped lines.** Anything out of stock at picking is dropped and not invoiced. You were authorised for the full amount but only charged for what shipped, so the invoice is lower than the order.

**Part dispatch.** The remaining lines are on a separate invoice that may not exist yet.

**Price at dispatch.** Lines added when editing an order are priced at the time of the edit, not the original order.

**Weight-variable lines.** Cheese, seafood, and charcuterie sold by weight are invoiced at actual picked weight, not the nominal weight shown online. A wheel of cheese sold at "approx 3kg" is charged at what it actually weighed. Small differences here are normal.

## Statements, for credit-terms accounts

A statement is not an invoice.
It is a summary of every invoice and credit note in the period, and it is what you pay against.

Statements are issued on the last working day of the month and are payable 30 days from that date.
Find them under **Invoices**, then **Statements**.

If you pay against individual invoices rather than the statement, use the invoice numbers as the payment reference or the payments will not match up.
