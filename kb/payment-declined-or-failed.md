# Payment declined or failed

## Why cards get declined

We are only told that a payment was declined, not why.
The reason stays between you and your card issuer, so we genuinely cannot look it up for you.

The usual causes, roughly in order of how often they turn out to be the answer:

- **Insufficient funds**, including a credit limit that is already reached.
- **The bank's fraud checks.** Wholesale food orders are larger than most card transactions and can look anomalous, especially on a first order or a new card.
- **Billing address mismatch.** The address on the card must match the billing address on your account exactly, including postcode formatting.
- **Expired card**, or a card reissued with a new expiry or security code.
- **3-D Secure not completed.** If the bank's verification step opened and you closed it, the payment fails silently.
- **International blocks**, where the card is not enabled for the merchant's country.

## What to do

Try the payment again first.
A meaningful share of declines are transient, particularly fraud-check declines that clear once the bank has seen you attempt it.

If it declines twice, **call your card issuer** rather than trying repeatedly.
Repeated attempts look like card testing and can get the card blocked outright.

The issuer can usually authorise the transaction while you are on the phone.

## Pending charges after a failed payment

A failed payment sometimes still leaves a pending charge on your statement.

This is an authorisation that was taken and then not completed.
It is not a real charge, no money has left your account, and it drops off within 3 to 5 working days on its own.

We cannot refund it, because there is nothing to refund.
If you retry the payment and it succeeds, you may briefly see two pending amounts. Only one becomes a real charge.

## Orders on hold after a failed payment

When payment fails, the order goes to **On hold** rather than being cancelled.
We hold it for **72 hours** so you have time to fix the payment without losing the order or the stock.

Sign in, go to **Orders**, open the order, and choose **Retry payment**.
You can use a different method.

After 72 hours the order is cancelled automatically and the stock is released.
Nothing is charged, but you have to place it again, and the price and availability may have changed.

## Payments that succeed but the order does not appear

Check the Orders page before doing anything else, and check any other account you have access to via the account switcher.

If money has left your account and there is genuinely no order, contact us with the amount, the date, and the last four digits of the card.
Do not simply place the order again, because you will very likely be charged twice.

## Credit-terms accounts

Orders on credit terms do not take a payment, so they cannot be declined.
They can go **On hold** if the order would exceed your credit limit.

That is not a payment failure and retrying will not help.
Settle part of the outstanding balance, or contact us to review the limit.
