# Requesting a refund

How to start a refund, and what we need from you.

## Starting a refund

Sign in, go to **Orders**, open the order, and choose **Request refund**.

Select the lines you want refunded and the reason.
The reason matters: it decides whether you pay the return carriage, and for perishables it decides whether the refund is possible at all.

You get a refund reference in the format `RF-` followed by six digits, for example `RF-208841`.
Use it in any correspondence about the refund.

## What we need

**For ambient goods you are returning:** nothing beyond the request. We email you a returns label.

**For damaged, faulty, or wrong goods:** photographs, uploaded with the request. We need to see the damage, the product label, and for perishables the temperature indicator on the packaging.

Without photographs a damage claim cannot be assessed and will be rejected, so do not skip this step even if the damage seems self-evident.
Take the photographs before you throw anything away.

## Do not destroy the goods

It is tempting to bin a leaking box straight away.
Do not.

Photograph the consignment as it arrived, including the outer packaging, before you unpack or dispose of anything.
For perishables that have failed, we will normally tell you to dispose of them, but we need to authorise that first.
Disposing of goods before we authorise it can invalidate the claim.

## Returning ambient goods

Once the refund is approved you get a prepaid returns label if the goods were faulty, or a returns address if you are returning them by choice.

Pack them in the original outer packaging where you can.
Write the refund reference `RF-...` on the outside of the box, because a return that arrives without a reference sits in goods-in until someone works out whose it is, which adds days.

## Timescales

| Stage | Typical time |
|---|---|
| Request acknowledged | Within 1 working day |
| Damage claim assessed | 2 to 3 working days |
| Returned goods inspected | 2 working days from receipt |
| Refund issued | 1 working day after approval |
| Money back with you | 3 to 5 working days after issue |

## Refunds without a return

For perishables that have failed, and for low-value damaged items, we usually refund without asking you to return anything, because the return carriage costs more than the goods.

If we do this you will be told to dispose of the goods.
You do not need to send anything back, and the refund proceeds straight to approval.

## Tracking it

Once the refund exists you can follow it.
See the article on tracking your refund.
