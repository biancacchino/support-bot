# Resetting your password

## Resetting it

Go to the sign-in page and choose **Forgot password**.
Enter the email address on the account and we send you a reset link.

The link is valid for **60 minutes** and can only be used once.
If you request several resets, only the most recent link works, because each new request invalidates the previous one.
This is the usual reason a link says it has expired when you only just received it: you clicked the older email.

## The reset email has not arrived

For security we send the same confirmation message whether or not the address is on an account, so "we have sent you a link" does not confirm that the account exists.

Check your spam folder first.

If nothing arrives within 15 minutes, the likely reason is that the address is not the one on the account.
Business accounts are often registered to a shared mailbox such as `accounts@` or `orders@` rather than to a personal address.
Try those.

If you genuinely cannot work out which address the account uses, contact us with the business name and account number.

## Password requirements

- At least 12 characters.
- Not a password you have used on this account before.
- Not one of the commonly breached passwords we check against.

We do not require a mixture of symbols, digits, and cases.
A long passphrase is both stronger and easier to remember than a short cryptic one, so length is what we enforce.

## You cannot access the registered email

This happens when the person who opened the account has left the business.

Contact us with your business name and account number.
We will ask you to confirm details we already hold, and for a request on company letterhead or from a verifiable company domain.

We are deliberately strict here.
An attacker taking over a trade account with credit terms can order goods on your credit, so the checks exist to protect you.
Expect this to take a couple of working days rather than minutes.

## Your account is locked

Ten failed sign-in attempts locks the account for 30 minutes.

Waiting it out works.
Successfully resetting your password also clears the lock immediately, so if you are locked out and in a hurry, reset rather than wait.

Repeated lockouts on an account you are not trying to access mean somebody else is trying to.
Tell us if that happens, and change the password.

## Staying signed in

Sessions last 30 days on a device you mark as trusted, and until you close the browser otherwise.

Changing your password signs out every other device immediately.
If you think somebody else has access to the account, changing the password is the fastest way to remove them.
