# Returns and damaged goods

What to do when a consignment arrives broken, short, warm, or wrong.

## Before you sign

You are entitled to inspect a consignment before you accept it, and it is worth doing.

If the outer packaging is visibly damaged, or a chilled box is not cold to the touch, you have two options:

- **Refuse the whole consignment.** It goes straight back and is refunded in full. Nothing else to do.
- **Accept it but sign as damaged.** Write "damaged" next to your signature, or on the driver's handheld device. This preserves the claim.

Signing clean for a consignment that is visibly damaged makes the claim much harder, because the courier's record says it arrived in good order.
If in doubt, sign as damaged. It costs nothing and commits you to nothing.

## Damaged goods

Photograph everything before you unpack: the outer box, the damage, the product labels, and the delivery note.

Then raise a refund request against the order, selecting **Damaged in transit** as the reason and uploading the photographs.

We assess in 2 to 3 working days.
For damaged goods we pay the return carriage, and for anything low-value we will usually just refund it and tell you to dispose of it rather than pay to ship a broken box back.

## Short deliveries

If a line is missing entirely, check the delivery note first.
Lines that were out of stock are shown as short-shipped and you were not charged for them, so this is not a missing item.

If the delivery note says a line was sent and it is not in the box, tell us within 3 working days.
Include the delivery note, since the discrepancy between what it says and what arrived is the evidence.

## Wrong goods

If you have been sent something you did not order, do not open it.
Raise a refund request with **Wrong item sent** as the reason.

We collect it and send the correct item, and you are not charged for either the collection or the redelivery.
An unopened wrong item is straightforward. An opened one becomes a judgement call.

## Perishables that arrive warm

This is the urgent one, because the claim window is only 24 hours.

Every chilled and frozen consignment ships with a temperature indicator on the inside of the box.
If it has been triggered, the cold chain has failed.

Photograph the indicator, the product, and the packaging, then raise the claim the same day.
Do not eat it, do not sell it on, and do not throw it away until we have authorised disposal.

We refund these in full and do not ask for the goods back.

## What happens to your refund

Once the claim is approved it follows the normal refund process.
See the article on tracking your refund for how long the money takes to come back, which depends mostly on how you paid.
