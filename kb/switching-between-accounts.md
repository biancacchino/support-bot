# Switching between accounts

If you buy for more than one business or site, one sign-in can reach several Northwind accounts.

## Switching

When your sign-in has access to more than one account, an account switcher appears in the top right, next to your name.
Select the account you want and the whole site switches to it: basket, orders, invoices, pricing, and delivery addresses.

Each account keeps its own basket.
A basket you built under one account is still there when you switch back, which is useful, and occasionally confusing if you forget which account you were buying for.

## Knowing which account you are in

The account name is always shown in the top right.

**Check it before you check out.**
The most common support issue with multi-account access is an order placed on the wrong account: the goods go to the wrong site, the invoice goes to the wrong entity, and if you are on credit terms it draws down the wrong credit limit.

Once an order is placed it cannot be moved between accounts.
It has to be cancelled and placed again, which is free before picking starts and may carry a fee after.
So the check costs you two seconds and the mistake costs you a cancellation fee.

## Getting access to another account

You cannot add yourself to an account.
The owner of that account has to invite you, from **Account settings**, then **Users**.

Once invited, the account appears in your switcher next to any others you already have.
You keep one set of sign-in credentials regardless of how many accounts you can reach.

## Different roles on different accounts

Your role is per account, not global.

You might be an Owner on one account and a Buyer on another, and the interface changes accordingly.
If a menu you expect is missing, check which account you are currently in before assuming something is broken. It is usually this.

## Leaving an account

Remove yourself from an account in **Account settings**, then **Users**.
You cannot remove yourself if you are the only Owner. Transfer ownership first, or the account would be left with nobody able to administer it.

## One person, one sign-in

Do not share sign-in credentials between people so they can access an account.
Invite them as their own user instead.

Shared credentials mean order history shows one name for actions taken by several people, and when somebody leaves you have to reset a password that everybody uses rather than simply removing one user.
