# Tracking your refund

Where your money is, and why it is taking as long as it is.

## Where to look

Sign in and go to **Orders**, then **Refunds**.
Every refund shows its reference (`RF-208841`), its status, the amount, and the date it changed status.

## Refund statuses

**Requested.**
We have your request and have not yet looked at it.
Sits here up to 1 working day.

**Awaiting goods.**
We are waiting for you to return the items.
This status will not move until the goods reach us, so if you have not sent them, this is why it is stuck.

**Awaiting evidence.**
We need photographs and do not have them.
Like the above, this will sit indefinitely until you act. We email you when a refund enters this state.

**Under review.**
We have everything and are assessing the claim.
2 to 3 working days, longer for a large or complex consignment.

**Approved.**
The refund is agreed and queued to be paid.

**Issued.**
We have sent the money back to your original payment method.
The clock is now with your bank, not with us.

**Rejected.**
The claim did not meet the refund policy.
The rejection email says why. If you disagree, reply to it and it goes to a human.

**Partially approved.**
Some lines refunded, some not, most often when part of a consignment was damaged and part was fine.

## Why it says Issued but the money is not there

This is the most common refund question and it almost never indicates a problem.

Once a refund is **Issued** we have released it.
How long it then takes to appear depends on your payment method:

| Method | Time after Issued |
|---|---|
| Debit or credit card | 3 to 5 working days |
| PayPal | Up to 24 hours |
| Bank transfer | 2 to 3 working days |
| Account credit | Immediate |
| 30-day terms | Applied to your next statement, not paid out |

Card refunds are the slow ones, and there is nothing we can do to speed them up.
The delay is entirely on the card issuer's side.

If it has been more than 5 working days since **Issued** on a card refund, contact your bank first, quoting the date.
If the bank has nothing, come back to us and we will provide the acquirer reference, which is what the bank will ask for.

## Refunds on 30-day terms

If your account is on credit terms, a refund is not paid out as money.
It is credited against your account and reduces what you owe on your next statement.

You will see a credit note rather than a payment.
This catches people out, so check your statement before assuming a refund has not been processed.

## Stuck refunds

**Awaiting goods** and **Awaiting evidence** never resolve themselves.
Both are waiting on you.
If a refund has not moved in a week, check it is not sitting in one of those two states.
